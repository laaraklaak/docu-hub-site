Why We Delete Your Documents in 30 Days.

Explaining our "Dual-Clock" Retention Strategy.

In the digital age, most people think "good service" means keeping client files forever "just in case." At Docu-Hub, we believe security means deleting data when it's no longer needed.

If a bearer keeps a photo of your Passport on their phone for 2 years, what happens when they lose that phone? Your identity is compromised.

To prevent this, we operate under a strict Dual-Clock Strategy, as outlined in our Privacy Policy.

Clock 1: The 30-Day Purge

Status: DELETE

What goes here: Scans of your Birth Certificate, ID photos, draft application forms, and initial emails.

Why? Once the government has your application, we have no legal reason to hold your sensitive biographic data. It is permanently wiped from our working servers 30 days after job completion.

Clock 2: The 7-Year Archive

Status: SECURE VAULT

What goes here: Your signed Service Agreement, Bearer Authorization Letter, and the Final Transaction Receipt.

Why? Financial laws (POCA) require us to prove we verified your identity and paid your taxes for 7 years. These files are moved to "Cold Storage" and are not accessible for daily operations.

This separation ensures that if our daily working folders were ever breached, your permanent data wouldn't be there. We minimize the risk by minimizing the data.

That is the difference between a "Bearer" and a Data Controller.
