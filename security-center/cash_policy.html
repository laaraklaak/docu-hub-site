Why We Don't Accept Bags of Cash.

Understanding the "Reimbursement Model" and why electronic transfers protect you.

At Docu-Hub, we occasionally get asked: "Can I just meet you and give you the cash for my Property Tax?"

The answer is always a polite, firm No.

This isn't because we are difficult. It is because we operate as a Designated Non-Financial Business (DNFBP) under Jamaican Law. Here is why our strict electronic payment policy is actually your best protection.

1. The Anti-Money Laundering Shield (POCA)

Under the Proceeds of Crime Act (POCA), businesses that handle large transactions are required to verify the source of funds. Accepting untraceable cash puts both the agency and the client at risk of scrutiny. By requiring bank transfers, we create an automatic, bank-verified audit trail that satisfies the Financial Investigations Division (FID).

2. The "Pass-Through" Protection

When you pay us for a service like a Passport Renewal, your money is split into two buckets (as detailed in our Terms of Service):

  • Service Fee: Our revenue.
  • Statutory Disbursement: The government's money.

We treat your tax money as "Trust Funds." Electronic payments allow us to clearly separate these funds in our accounting, ensuring that 100% of the money meant for the government actually goes to the government.

3. Your "Bulletproof" Receipt

Cash receipts can be lost or disputed. A bank transfer is permanent proof.

The Audit Trail Guarantee

If a government agency ever claims you didn't pay your Fitness Fee, we can pull the exact transaction ID from our bank ledger and prove it within minutes. Cash doesn't offer that security.

So, while it might take an extra minute to log into your banking app, that minute buys you total security, legal compliance, and peace of mind.
