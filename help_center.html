How can we make it simpler?

Most government portals are designed for administrators. We designed Docu-Hub for people. Use the guides below to navigate the red tape.

Ready to start? Check these first:

1. Your Documents

  • Original Birth Certificate
  • Valid Government ID
  • TRN Number / Slip

2. Digital Photos

  • Clear, high-res ID photos
  • Natural lighting (No flash glare)
  • Ears clearly visible

3. Signatures

  • Be ready to sign our digital pad
  • JP contact (if minor applicant)

The Requirement Decoder

Agency says:

"Submit biographic documentation in 600 DPI PDF format."

Docu-Hub Decoder:

Send us a clear, high-resolution photo of your ID. Just ensure there is no camera flash glare on the plastic.

Agency says:

"Statutory authorization via Bearer's Letter required."

Docu-Hub Decoder:

Sign the digital signature pad in our intake form. We generate the formal letter for you instantly.

Common Roadblocks

Why was my passport photo rejected elsewhere?
PICA is strict about shadows, glasses, and hair covering ears. Our intake officers pre-vet your photo before submission. If we see a problem, weâ€™ll tell you before you pay.
I lost my TRN slip. Can you find it?
Yes. We don't need the slip. We just need your full legal name and birth date to perform a search and retrieve your official Taxpayer ID number.
I live overseas. How do I pay statutory fees?
We accept international bank transfers (NCB). Once funds clear, we pay the local agency in JMD on your behalf and provide the official government receipt.
